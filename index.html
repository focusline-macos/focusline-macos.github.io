On reading slowly

There is a particular kind of tiredness that comes not from reading too much, but from reading without an anchor. The eye drifts, the line is lost, and the same sentence is read three times before the mind catches up.

A guide changes that. A quiet line under the words gives the eye something to rest against, and the page stops sliding. Dim the rest of the screen and the world narrows to a single thought at a time.

None of this is new — it is how a finger under the line has always worked. Focusline simply lends you one that never tires, in whatever shape and colour reads best to you.

Try it: glide down this paragraph and watch the guide keep pace. Change the Mode in the app panel, pick a colour, widen the band. Make it yours.

Four Modes

One tap sets everything up.

Each Mode pairs a reading guide with a screen tint, tuned to a job. Switch from the menu bar or a keyboard shortcut.

📖

Reading

A clear line guides every line you read — thin bar or wide highlight, your colour, your width.

🪟

Focus

Everything but your app fades away. The page you're on stays bright; the clutter behind it dims.

🔦

Spotlight

A spotlight on the line you're reading. Round, square, or a full-width band — it follows your cursor.

🌙

Night

A warm, dim wash that's easy on the eyes — low glare and less blue light for late reading.

🎚️

Questions

Does Focusline see what's on my screen?

No. It reads only your cursor position so the guide can follow it. It has no way of knowing what's under the bar or on the page.

Will it work in any app?

Yes. The guide and tint are a passive overlay drawn on top of everything, so they work in your browser, PDF reader, editor — anywhere.

What does it need?

macOS 13 or later. Focusline runs sandboxed in your menu bar.

Is my data collected?

Focusline uses privacy-respecting analytics and crash reporting to improve the app. No reading content or screen contents are ever collected. See the Privacy Policy.
